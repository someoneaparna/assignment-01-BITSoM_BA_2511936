## Architecture Recommendation

I would recommend a Data Lakehouse for this food delivery startup. The company is collecting highly varied data types: structured payment transactions, semi-structured GPS logs, unstructured customer reviews, and restaurant menu images. A classic data warehouse is too rigid for that variety, while a pure data lake can become difficult to govern and query consistently as the business scales. A lakehouse gives the startup the flexibility of low-cost object storage with the reliability and analytical structure needed for production reporting and machine learning.

The first reason is multimodal storage. GPS streams, text, tabular transactions, and images can all land in the same storage foundation without heavy up-front modeling. The second reason is analytics readiness. The startup will eventually need dashboards for delivery times, refund rates, regional demand, and restaurant performance. A lakehouse supports SQL analytics directly on curated tables while still preserving raw data for reprocessing. The third reason is machine learning support. Customer reviews and menu images are especially useful for recommendation systems, fraud checks, and quality scoring. Keeping raw and refined data together makes feature engineering and experimentation easier.

A fourth practical reason is governance. Lakehouse table formats provide schema evolution, ACID-style table operations, and better data quality controls than a loosely managed data lake. That matters once multiple teams begin consuming the same data. In short, a Data Lakehouse is the best fit because it balances flexibility, scale, analytics performance, and governance without forcing the startup to choose between raw ingestion and structured decision-making.
